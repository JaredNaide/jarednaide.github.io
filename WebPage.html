Jared Naide's Home Page

Hi! My name is Jared Naide. I live in the great city of Athens, but I am originally from Johns Creek, Georgia. I have lived in Johns Creek my entire life, and grew up there with my mother, father, and sister. For most of my childhood, my family was really happy and satisfied with our ordinary lifestyle. My father and I would engage in more active activities such as tennis, basketball, bike riding, and video gaming. My mother, on the other hand,tried to enrich my learning with the arts, modern culture, and history. I loved growing up with both of them because they always seemed to balance each another out. However, my parents split up when I was 14. To be candid, I was devastated and hurt by my parents. While this was an extremely difficult change in my life at the time, I was able to adjust. As I matured and grew older, I learned and understood why it had to happen. And now, we have all moved on. My mother is remarried with a new husband, and my father is moving to DC for his new job. One individual who really helped me during the split was my sister (who is pictured above) as she truly understood everything that I was experiencing. Although my sister is 3 years older than me,we are extremely close. In fact, she was the one who inspired me to apply to UGA as that was where she enrolled. She graduated this past year and has found professional success with a new internship. While we may have suffered hardship as a result of the divorce, I am so glad that now we are all in a much better place.

As for myself, I am an aspiring double major with psychology and intended marketing as my areas of interest. When I first arrived at UGA, my advisor suggested I pick my major in her wordsASAP. However, it took me a while to decide on my majors of interest as there are many areas of study to choose from. Back when I was at Johns Creek High School, I wound up taking a variety of courses as I had passion for multiple subjects With my two selected majors, I finally feel that my academic and professional careers have direction. During freshmen year, I lived in Brumby Hall. Although freshmen year will always be my favorite and the most memorable, I am extremely thankful to have moved out of Brumby. This year, I live in Tau Epsilon Phi, which is a fraternity house located in Greek Circle. Surprisingly, the house is much cleaner in comparison to Brumby. My social life this year has flourished with having date nights, socials, and formals. In addition, I have been able to maintain my work ethic while living in-house. Having the opportunity to live in Greek Circle has been amazing, and I am so fortunate to have such incredible friends and brothers. While it'll be very upsetting to leave, I am excited to live in Whistlebury next year with some of my closest friends.
